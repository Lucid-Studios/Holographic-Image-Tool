# DCO and CLA Policy

## Default Rule

Lucid Technologies uses a **Developer Certificate of Origin style sign-off requirement** for normal code, documentation, schema, and test contributions.

Each commit should include a sign-off line:

```text
Signed-off-by: Your Name <you@example.com>
```

Recommended command:

```powershell
git commit -s
```

## Contributor Attestation

By signing off a commit, the contributor attests that:

- they wrote the contribution or otherwise have the right to submit it
- the contribution may be included in this repository
- they are not knowingly submitting secrets, restricted data, or unauthorized third-party material
- they have disclosed material AI assistance when required by repository policy

## CLA Escalation Rule

Lucid Technologies may require a separate CLA or equivalent written grant before merge for:

- large external contributions
- contributions submitted on behalf of an employer or institution
- contributions involving novel datasets, model assets, or media rights
- contributions with unclear ownership or licensing provenance
- contributions maintainers believe create unusual intellectual-property risk

## Current Repository Operating Mode

- DCO-style sign-off is the default expectation
- CLA is **case-by-case**, not universally required yet
- maintainers may pause review until authorship and rights are clarified

## Maintainer Review

Maintainers should check:

- whether commits are signed off
- whether authorship appears consistent with the contribution
- whether any third-party source or AI tool involvement needs explicit disclosure
- whether escalated review or a CLA request is necessary
